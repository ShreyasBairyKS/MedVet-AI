# Sample Medical Knowledge Base - Common Dog Conditions

## Limping and Lameness

### Causes:
- Sprains or strains (most common)
- Arthritis (especially in older dogs)
- Hip dysplasia
- Torn ligaments (ACL/CCL tears)
- Paw pad injuries
- Bone fractures

### Symptoms to watch for:
- Reluctance to put weight on limb
- Swelling around joints
- Limping that worsens with activity
- Yelping when touched

### Recommended tests:
- Physical examination
- X-rays for bone issues
- Joint manipulation tests
- Blood tests for inflammatory markers

### Treatment approaches:
- Rest and limited activity (mild cases)
- NSAIDs for pain/inflammation (vet prescribed)
- Physical therapy
- Surgery for severe cases (torn ligaments, fractures)

### Urgency:
- RED: Complete inability to bear weight, visible deformity
- YELLOW: Progressive worsening, significant pain
- GREEN: Mild limp, no other symptoms

## Skin Conditions

### Common canine dermatology issues:
- Allergic dermatitis
- Hot spots
- Fungal infections
- Bacterial infections
- Parasites (fleas, mites)

### Visual indicators:
- Redness and inflammation
- Hair loss
- Crusty or scaly patches
- Excessive scratching or licking

### Diagnostic steps:
- Skin scraping
- Fungal culture
- Allergy testing
- Biopsy (severe cases)

## Gastrointestinal Issues

### Symptoms:
- Vomiting
- Diarrhea
- Loss of appetite
- Lethargy

### Common causes:
- Dietary indiscretion
- Food allergies
- Parasites
- Inflammatory bowel disease
- Foreign body obstruction

### When to seek immediate care:
- Bloody vomit or stool
- Severe dehydration
- Abdominal distension
- Continuous vomiting for 24+ hours
