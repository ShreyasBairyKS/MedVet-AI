# Human Dermatology Reference Guide

## Skin Lesion Types

### Moles and Nevi
- Benign pigmented lesions
- Usually symmetric, well-defined borders
- Consistent color (brown, tan, or skin-colored)
- Monitor for ABCDE changes (Asymmetry, Border irregularity, Color variation, Diameter >6mm, Evolution)

### Melanoma Warning Signs (ABCDE)
- **A**symmetry: One half doesn't match the other
- **B**order: Irregular, scalloped, or poorly defined edges
- **C**olor: Multiple colors or uneven distribution
- **D**iameter: Larger than 6mm (pencil eraser)
- **E**volution: Changes in size, shape, or color

### Eczema/Dermatitis
- Red, inflamed patches
- Itchy and may weep clear fluid
- Often on flexural areas (elbows, knees)
- Can be triggered by allergens, irritants, or stress

### Psoriasis
- Thick, scaly, silvery plaques
- Commonly on elbows, knees, scalp
- May have nail involvement
- Chronic autoimmune condition

## Acne and Rosacea

### Acne vulgaris:
- Comedones (blackheads/whiteheads)
- Inflammatory papules and pustules
- Most common in teenagers, but can persist in adults
- Requires topical or oral treatments

### Rosacea:
- Facial redness, especially cheeks and nose
- Visible blood vessels
- May have papules/pustules (often mistaken for acne)
- Triggers: alcohol, spicy food, sun exposure

## Skin Infections

### Bacterial (Cellulitis, Impetigo)
- Warm, red, swollen area
- May have pus-filled blisters
- Requires antibiotic treatment

### Fungal (Ringworm, Candida)
- Circular, scaly patches with raised borders
- Can spread to others
- Antifungal treatment needed

### Viral (Warts, Herpes)
- Warts: small, rough bumps
- Herpes: painful blisters in clusters
- Often resolve on their own, but antivirals help

## Recommended Actions by Condition

### Immediate medical attention:
- Rapidly growing or changing lesions
- Bleeding or non-healing wounds
- Signs of infection (fever, spreading redness)
- Severe allergic reactions

### Schedule appointment soon:
- New or changing moles
- Persistent rash >2 weeks
- Painful skin conditions

### Home care acceptable:
- Minor dry skin
- Mild acne
- Known eczema with usual treatment
