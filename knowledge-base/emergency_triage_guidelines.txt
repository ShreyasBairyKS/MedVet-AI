# Emergency Triage Guidelines - Medical and Veterinary

## RED - IMMEDIATE CARE REQUIRED (Life-threatening)

### Humans:
- Chest pain with shortness of breath
- Severe difficulty breathing
- Uncontrolled bleeding
- Loss of consciousness
- Stroke symptoms (FAST: Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 911)
- Severe allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)
- Major trauma or head injury
- Seizures

### Animals:
- Difficulty breathing or choking
- Uncontrollable bleeding
- Unable to urinate (especially male cats)
- Seizures lasting >5 minutes
- Severe bloat (GDV - especially large dogs)
- Collapse or inability to stand
- Severe trauma
- Toxin ingestion (confirmed or suspected)
- Heatstroke

## YELLOW - URGENT CARE (Within 24 hours)

### Humans:
- High fever (>103°F / 39.4°C)
- Severe vomiting/diarrhea with dehydration
- Moderate injuries requiring stitches
- Severe pain not controlled by OTC medications
- Sudden vision changes
- Persistent bleeding
- Signs of infection (redness, warmth, pus)

### Animals:
- Vomiting/diarrhea for >24 hours
- Not eating for 24+ hours
- Lethargy or behavior changes
- Difficulty urinating
- Eye injuries or sudden vision loss
- Limping with no weight bearing
- Open wounds
- Persistent coughing

## GREEN - NON-URGENT (Can wait 2-3 days or schedule routine visit)

### Humans:
- Minor cold/flu symptoms
- Mild rash without fever
- Minor cuts/scrapes
- Mild sprains
- Routine medication refills
- Preventive care

### Animals:
- Mild limping that improves with rest
- Minor scratches
- Mild digestive upset (1-2 episodes)
- Routine vaccinations
- Mild skin irritation
- Ear cleaning needs

## Special Considerations

### Pediatric (human) urgency modifiers:
- Infants <3 months with ANY fever: RED
- Dehydration in children: YELLOW
- Fever with rash: YELLOW

### Geriatric (human/animal) urgency modifiers:
- Lower threshold for urgent care
- Falls in elderly humans: YELLOW
- Sudden behavior changes in senior pets: YELLOW

### Breed-specific (veterinary):
- Brachycephalic breeds (pugs, bulldogs): breathing issues = RED
- Large/giant breeds: bloat symptoms = RED
- Toy breeds: hypoglycemia risk = YELLOW

## Pain Scale Reference

### 0-3: Mild (GREEN)
- Minor discomfort
- Not affecting daily activities
- OTC pain relief appropriate

### 4-6: Moderate (YELLOW)
- Noticeable discomfort
- Affecting some activities
- May need prescription pain management

### 7-10: Severe (RED)
- Extreme pain
- Unable to function normally
- Immediate medical attention needed
